Why I like grid frameworks

Choose a workplace:

You are in constructions, a builder working with scaffolding...

The vast majority of websites and applications use a grid. There may be not an explicit grid system in place but a simple two-column layout with a sidebar and a main content area is already a grid.

We actually invent a new grid system around the product. When the layout changes a bit though, sometimes it's not easy to maintain the initial stability.

Grid frameworks such as Bootstrap's grid system are there to manage a more complex layouts

The grid is the mannequin and the comp is the pattern.

Working with grids allows designers and programmers to easily communicate and understand how to put webpages together. It provides developers with units of measurement that they can easily turn into divs.

It also informs both sides how to layout elements on a page so that they can transition between mobile and desktop.

Benefits of grid frameworks

  • Visual harmony

  • A balanced and consistently implemented design scheme will increase readers’ confidence

  • Provides coherence to complexity

  • Easy maintenance

  • Communication developers/designers, smoother workflow

  • Responsive design

The goal is to establish a consistent, logical screen layout, one that allows us to “plug in” text and graphics without having to stop and rethink our basic design approach on each new page.

coherence to complexity: it's a matter of counting until 12.

easy maintenance: it's outside our code but always available.

communication: we sometimes communicate to each other with two slightly different unit of measurements, that leads to misunderstandings or pitfalls.
